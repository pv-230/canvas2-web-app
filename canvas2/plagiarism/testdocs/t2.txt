We the People of the United States, to frame a more wonderful Union, lay out Justice, protect homegrown Tranquility, accommodate the normal guard, advance the overall Welfare, and secure the Blessings of Liberty to ourselves and our Posterity, do appoint and lay out this Constitution for the United States of America.

All regulative Powers in this allowed will be vested in a Congress of the United States, which will comprise of a Senate and House of Representatives.

The House of Representatives will be made out of Members selected consistently Year by the People of the few States, and the Electors in each State will have the Qualifications essential for Electors of the most various Branch of the State Legislature.

No Person will be a Representative who will not have achieved the Age of a quarter century, and been seven Years a Citizen of the United States, and who will not, when chosen, be an Inhabitant of that State in which he will be picked.

Agents and direct Taxes will be distributed among the few States which might be incorporated inside this Union, as per their particular Numbers, which still up in the air by adding to the entire Number of free Persons, including those bound to Service for a Term of Years, and barring Indians not burdened, three fifths of any remaining Persons. The genuine Enumeration will be made in the span of three Years after the principal Meeting of the Congress of the United States, and inside each ensuing Term of a decade, in such Manner as they will by Law direct. The Number of Representatives will not surpass one for each 30,000, yet each State will have something like one Representative; and until such list will be made, the State of New Hampshire will be qualified for chuse three, Massachusetts eight, Rhode-Island and Providence Plantations one, Connecticut five, New-York six, New Jersey four, Pennsylvania eight, Delaware one, Maryland six, Virginia ten, North Carolina five, South Carolina five, and Georgia three.

At the point when opportunities occur in the Representation from any State, the Executive Authority thereof will give Writs of Election to fill such Vacancies.

The House of Representatives will chuse their Speaker and different Officers; and will have the sole Power of Impeachment.

The Senate of the United States will be made out of two Senators from each State, picked by the Legislature thereof, for a long time; and every Senator will have one Vote.

Following they will be collected in Consequence of the primary Election, they will be isolated as similarly as might be into three Classes. The Seats of the Senators of the top of the line will be abandoned at the Expiration of the subsequent Year, of the below average at the Expiration of the fourth Year, and of the second rate Class at the Expiration of the 6th Year, so 33% might be selected consistently Year; and assuming Vacancies occur by Resignation, or in any case, during the Recess of the Legislature of any State, the Executive thereof may make transitory Appointments until the following Meeting of the Legislature, which will then fill such Vacancies.

No Person will be a Senator who will not have accomplished the Age of thirty Years, and been nine Years a Citizen of the United States, and who will not, when chosen, be an Inhabitant of that State for which he will be picked.

The Vice President of the United States will be President of the Senate, yet will have no Vote, except if they be similarly separated.

The Senate will chuse their different Officers, and furthermore a President genius tempore, in the Absence of the Vice President, or when he will practice the Office of President of the United States.

The Senate will have the sole Power to attempt all Impeachments. While sitting for that Purpose, they will be on Oath or Affirmation. At the point when the President of the United States is attempted, the Chief Justice will manage: And no Person will be sentenced without the Concurrence of 66% of the Members present.

Judgment in Cases of Impeachment will not stretch out farther than to expulsion from Office, and exclusion to hold and partake in any Office of high standing, Trust or Profit under the United States: yet the Party sentenced will by and by be responsible and dependent upon Indictment, Trial, Judgment and Punishment, as indicated by Law.

The Times, Places and Manner of holding Elections for Senators and Representatives, will be endorsed in each State by the Legislature thereof; yet the Congress may out of the blue by Law make or change such Regulations, besides with regards to the Places of chusing Senators.

The Congress will gather no less than once in consistently, and such Meeting will be on the main Monday in December, except if they will by Law choose an alternate Day.

Each House will be the Judge of the Elections, Returns and Qualifications of its own Members, and a Majority of each will comprise a Quorum to carry on with Work; however a more modest Number might defer from one day to another, and might be approved to force the Attendance of missing Members, in such Manner, and under such Penalties as each House might give.

Each House might decide the Rules of its Proceedings, rebuff its Members for misconduct, and, with the Concurrence of 66%, oust a Member.

Each House will keep a Journal of its Proceedings, and now and again distribute something very similar, with the exception of such Parts as may in their Judgment require Secrecy; and the Yeas and Nays of the Members of one or the other House on any inquiry will, at the Desire of one fifth of those Present, be placed on the Journal.

Neither House, during the Session of Congress, will, without the Consent of the other, suspend for over three days, nor to some other Place than that where the two Houses will sit.

The Senators and Representatives will get a Compensation for their Services, to be found out by Law, and paid out of the Treasury of the United States. They will in all Cases, with the exception of Treason, Felony and Breach of the Peace, be favored from Arrest during their Attendance at the Session of their individual Houses, and in going to and getting back from something similar; and for any Speech or Debate in one or the other House, they will not be addressed in some other Place.

No Senator or Representative will, during the Time for which he was chosen, be delegated to any thoughtful Office under the Authority of the United States, which will have been made, or the Emoluments whereof will have been encreased during such time; and no Person holding any Office under the United States, will be a Member of one or the other House during his Continuance in Office.
